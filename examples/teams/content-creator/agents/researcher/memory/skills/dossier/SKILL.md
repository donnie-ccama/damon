# Dossier

Your one job: turn `00-brief.md` into `10-research.md` — a **truth-and-texture
dossier** for a single AI use case. Remember the reframe: **none of this will be
cited in the essay.** The finished piece bans statistics, studies, citations, and
jargon. Everything here feeds truthful *illustration*, not footnotes.

Priorities, in order: **accuracy over volume**, **jargon-free phrasing**, and
**explicit uncertainty flags**. A short, true, vivid dossier beats a long one.

---

## Research method (step by step)

1. **Read the brief.** Open
   `/Users/donnielane/cortado/teams/content-creator/pipeline/<job-slug>/00-brief.md`.
   Confirm the single AI use case, the target reader moment, and the candidate
   counter-intuitive truth. If the brief is missing or incomplete, **stop and say
   so** — do not invent it.
2. **Pin the mechanics.** In plain language, nail what the tool/workflow actually
   does. Use web search **only** to verify accuracy — never to collect numbers or
   authorities to quote. If you can't confirm something, mark it uncertain.
3. **Build the parallels.** Find 2–3 analogies to Excel / CRM / QuickBooks that a
   non-technical leader already understands. Test each: would someone fluent in
   spreadsheets but not in AI actually feel the click of recognition?
4. **Collect recognizable moments.** Write 2–3 true-to-life scenarios the reader
   would instantly know — grounded in real small-business / nonprofit life.
5. **Name the common story, then flip it.** State the tired conventional take, then
   the one surprising truth that overturns it (tie back to the brief's candidate).
6. **Steelman the skeptic.** Write the hardest honest objection a tired realist
   would raise — the real one, not a strawman. The encouragement has to survive it.
7. **Get concrete about Tuesday.** Describe hour-to-hour what adopting this looks
   like on a normal workday — embarrassingly specific, no gloss.
8. **Offer metaphor candidates.** Suggest 2–3 image systems the Writer could carry
   across the whole piece. Keep them fresh, not clichéd.
9. **Seed one pull-quote.** Draft one screenshot-worthy line the Writer might adapt.
10. **Sweep for jargon and fabrication.** Reread. Rewrite any term the reader
    wouldn't know. Flag anything you couldn't verify. Then write the file below.

## Reminders

- **Accuracy over volume.** Cut anything you can't stand behind.
- **Jargon-free everywhere**, including suggested phrasings.
- **Flag uncertainty explicitly** — the ⚠️ marks and the Uncertainty flags section.
- **Never fabricate AI capabilities.** If the tool can't do it, say the tool can't.
- Write **only** `10-research.md`. Never skip a stage or touch another file.
