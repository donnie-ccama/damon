# Homiletical Synthesis (the shared spine)

## Overview

You compress a 20-section exegetical study into one **audience-neutral spine**: the
few things every one of the three briefs must share. This is not a sermon and not a
brief — it is the skeleton that keeps the Theologian, Practitioner, and Skeptic
briefs saying the same true thing. Keep it tight, faithful, and reusable.

## The core principle

**Extract, don't editorialize.** Everything in the spine traces back to
`10-exegetical-study.md`. You are not adding insight; you are surfacing the study's
own load-bearing structure so the Editor doesn't have to re-read 20 sections to
stay faithful — and can't accidentally drift the three briefs apart.

## Steps

1. **Read** `00-brief.md` and, fully, `10-exegetical-study.md`. If the study is
   missing or incomplete, **stop and tell the Orchestrator**.
2. **Pull the central claim** — the single sentence the passage presses. Lean on
   the study's Biblical-theological synthesis (section 16) and summary (section 20).
3. **Trace the movements** — the 3–5 ordered beats of the passage's argument, from
   the structure/discourse and verse-by-verse sections. One plain sentence each.
4. **State the road to Christ** — from the study's Christological trajectory
   (section 19). State the *road* plainly; do not name the framework.
5. **Name the virtue/value** the passage surfaces and the **honest objection** — the
   hardest fair pushback a thoughtful reader would raise.
6. **Gather illustration seeds** — 2–3 concrete, real-life images the truth maps
   onto. Keep them audience-neutral (no pitch yet).
7. **List load-bearing details** — the few exegetical facts (a key term, a genre
   move, a historical note, a decided crux) no faithful brief may lose.
8. **Write** `20-synthesis.md` from the template. Hand off to the Orchestrator.

## The template (`20-synthesis.md`)

```
# Synthesis (spine) — <Passage>

## Central claim
<one sentence the passage presses>

## Movements
1. <beat one>
2. <beat two>
3. <beat three>
(… up to ~5)

## Road to Christ
<the legitimate trajectory, stated plainly — no framework names>

## The virtue / value
<what the passage asks of a life>

## The honest objection
<the hardest fair pushback the briefs must answer, not dodge>

## Illustration seeds (audience-neutral)
- <image 1>
- <image 2>
- <image 3>

## Load-bearing details (do not lose)
- <key term (transliteration) — what it does>
- <genre / discourse move that carries the meaning>
- <historical or canonical fact the reading depends on>
- <the decided crux(es) and their verdict>

## Uncertainties carried from the study
- <anything the study flagged uncertain — stays uncertain downstream>
```

## The two rules

- **Audience-neutral.** Do not write in the Theologian's register, the
  Practitioner's warmth, or the Skeptic's plainness. That is the Editor's job. Give
  raw, true material all three can draw from.
- **Never exceed the study.** No claim beyond `10-exegetical-study.md`. Carry its
  uncertainties forward verbatim in the final section.

## Common mistakes

- **Turning the spine into a mini-sermon.** It's a skeleton, not prose to preach.
- **Pre-baking one audience.** If a line only fits the Theologian (or only the
  Skeptic), it belongs in the brief, not the spine.
- **Adding insight the study didn't earn.** Surface; don't supplement.
