# Write the Three Audience Briefs

## Overview

You re-pitch one true message through three doors. The **central claim** and the
**road to Christ** are identical across all three briefs; the vocabulary, the
assumed knowledge, the kind of authority that persuades, and the illustrations all
change. Your binding specs live in
`/Users/donnielane/cortado/teams/sermon-prep/reference/audiences.md` — read it every
run; this skill is the working procedure, that file is the law.

## Inputs

- `20-synthesis.md` — the spine (your primary working source: central claim,
  movements, road to Christ, virtue, objection, illustration seeds).
- `10-exegetical-study.md` — the ceiling and the detail bank (cruxes, terms,
  advocates) — especially for The Theologian brief.
- `reference/audiences.md` — the three specs.

If the spine or the study is missing, **stop and tell the Orchestrator**.

## The one invariant

**One truth, three doors.** Before you write, fix the central claim and the road to
Christ from the spine. Those two do not change between briefs. If a brief needs to
change the *claim* to reach its reader, you have drifted — stop and re-pitch the
*delivery*, not the truth. **Never exceed the study**; carry its uncertainties into
all three briefs.

## Brief 1 — The Theologian (`30-theologian.md`)

**Reader:** Biblically literate, actively curious, scholarly. Grants the authority
of the text; wants the machinery.

**Do:**
- Keep original-language terms (with italic transliteration), discourse structure,
  genre, typology, canonical context.
- Engage the **cruxes** — name the live options with advocates and adjudicate,
  drawing from the study's sections 4–10 and 17.
- Situate the reading in the **scholarly dialogue**: where it sits against major
  alternatives and why.
- Name and defend the Christological road explicitly.

**Don't:** hand-hold, over-explain basics, or water down a defended crux.

**Length:** ~1200–1800 words (the fullest brief).

---

## Brief 2 — The Practitioner (`31-practitioner.md`)

**Reader:** Not Biblically literate, but open to Scripture's values. Grants that the
values are worth living; needs them made reachable.

**Do:**
- Translate each finding into **lived wisdom** — what it asks of an ordinary week.
- Keep **one** anchoring passage reference, quoted plainly and explained in a
  sentence. No chains of citations, no untranslated terms.
- Foreground the **virtue**, show what practicing it looks like, name its
  **counterfeit** and its honest **cost**.
- Land the road to Christ in plain, warm language.

**Don't:** use insider jargon; assume familiarity with the canon; moralize ("just
try harder").

**Length:** ~900–1300 words.

---

## Brief 3 — The Skeptic (`32-skeptic.md`)

**Reader:** Young, secular, 8th-grade vocabulary, grants **nothing** on authority.
Follows a point only when it is carried by **sound logic and a clear illustration**.
Not antagonistic — an honest equal at a table.

**Do:**
- Lead with a **clear illustration** the reader already knows from their own life,
  then draw the point out of it.
- Argue from **logic and lived experience first**. Earn every claim on the ground
  before naming any source.
- Use **short, plain sentences** and everyday words (8th-grade level).
- Name the **strongest objection** out loud and answer it fairly.
- You may mention the passage as *the source of an idea worth testing* — never as
  proof that settles the matter.

**Hard bans (any hit = rewrite):** "the Bible says / clearly teaches"; untranslated
Greek/Hebrew; assumed belief in God as a premise; religious jargon or insider
references; vocabulary above 8th grade; preaching, altar calls, or shaming.

**Length:** ~700–1100 words (the leanest brief).

## The brief template (adapt tone per audience)

```
# <The Theologian | The Practitioner | The Skeptic> — <Passage>

> Audience: <one line from reference/audiences.md>

<the brief, in that reader's voice — opening, the movements of the passage
carried in the reader's own terms, the honest objection answered, and the
landing (the road to Christ, pitched for this reader)>
```

## Self-check before every hand-off

For **each** brief:
- [ ] Central claim + road to Christ match the spine and the other two briefs.
- [ ] Asserts nothing beyond `10-exegetical-study.md`; uncertainties preserved.
- [ ] Matches its section of `reference/audiences.md` (vocabulary, assumed
      knowledge, kind of authority, length).
- [ ] No moralism at the landing.

For **The Skeptic** specifically:
- [ ] 8th-grade vocabulary throughout (read it back — any word that needs a
      seminary is cut).
- [ ] No claim rests on Biblical authority; each is earned by logic + illustration.
- [ ] No jargon, no untranslated original languages, no assumed belief.

## Revision (on an Orchestrator QA note)

Overwrite the one flagged brief in place; address every note against its audience
spec; hand back. Capped at **2 loops** per brief.

## Common mistakes

- **The three briefs drifting to three different claims.** Fix the claim first;
  vary only the door.
- **The Skeptic brief leaning on authority or jargon.** The most common failure —
  check it hardest, read it aloud at 8th-grade level.
- **The Theologian brief going soft, or the Practitioner brief going academic.**
  Each reader is failed by the other's register.
